Our Practice Areas

DWI Defense

Arrested for DWI? Here's what's at stake: your driver's license, your job, your record, and depending on the charge, your freedom. A conviction can mean jail time, fines, and a suspension that makes it hard to work. But a DWI arrest is not a DWI conviction, and you have more options than you think.

We attack these cases where they break: the reason for the stop, the reason it turned into a DWI investigation, the field sobriety tests, and the science behind the breath or blood evidence. At every step, we make sure your rights were respected, because a violation of your rights can change the entire case. If you drive for a living, the fight matters twice, because a DWI can put a CDL and the paycheck that comes with it at risk.

What most people don't realize is that a DWI arrest actually starts two cases against you: the criminal charge, and a separate case to suspend your license. You have only 15 days from your arrest to request the hearing that fights the suspension. Miss it, and it happens automatically.

Don't spend those days worrying. Spend one phone call finding out where you stand. The consultation is free.

Assault and Family Violence

Charged with assault or family violence? Here's what's at stake: your record, your job, your home, access to your family and your kids, and depending on the charge, your freedom. But a charge is not a conviction, and the story the police report tells is rarely the whole story.

Understand what a family violence finding actually does. It stays on your record permanently, it cannot be sealed or expunged, it takes away your right to own a firearm, it can be used against you in a custody case, and it turns a second accusation into a felony. That's why the goal isn't just avoiding jail. It's avoiding the finding itself.

Here's the situation we see all the time: the person who called 911 doesn't want to press charges anymore. Maybe they never did. But it's not their decision. Once the State picks up a family violence case, only the State decides whether it goes forward. That's why these cases have to be defended.

So we go get the whole story. The statements, the photos, the 911 call, the history between the people involved. We make every effort to interview everyone involved, including the complaining witness. At every step, we make sure your side is actually heard.

Whether the complaining witness wants to dismiss the case or not, you need someone in your corner who knows how these cases actually work. Don't wait for decisions to get made without you. One phone call tells you where you stand. The consultation is free.

Drug Charges

Charged with drug possession? Here's what's at stake: your record, your job, and depending on the charge, your freedom. But an arrest is just the State's opening move, and in drug cases, how the police came to find the evidence matters just as much as what they claim they found.

For a possession charge to hold up in court, the police had to get a lot of things right: the reason for the stop, the length of the stop, the reason for the search, where the evidence was found, how it was found, and whether the substance is even what the officers claim it is. We question everything. We review all the evidence, we demand the lab results, we check the chain of custody from the roadside to the courtroom, and when any of it falls short, we ask the court to act. One broken link, and the whole case can come apart.

Every drug case comes down to one of two fights: proving the State can't make its case, or showing them why they shouldn't. Whether that means attacking the evidence, telling your side of the story, or fighting for an outcome that keeps your record clean, you need someone who knows which fight to pick and how to win it. One phone call tells you where you stand. The consultation is free.

Theft Charges

Charged with theft? Here's what's at stake: your record, your job, your reputation, and for some, even your immigration status. Theft is a crime of dishonesty, and employers, landlords, and licensing boards treat it differently because it goes to whether you can be trusted. Under immigration law, it's considered a crime of moral turpitude, meaning even a misdemeanor conviction can affect your immigration status.

What most people don't realize is that in a theft case, the dollar amount decides the severity of the charge. The value the State puts on the property is what separates a ticket from a misdemeanor, and a misdemeanor from a felony.

To convict you, the State has to prove you knowingly took property that wasn't yours, without consent, intending to keep it, and every piece of that must be proven beyond a reasonable doubt. So we pull it all apart: the video, the receipts, the witness statements, and the value they claim.

The earlier we get involved, the more options stay open. Whether it's an allegation that you didn't pay for everything at checkout or an accusation involving thousands of dollars, you need someone who knows how these cases are fought and won. One phone call tells you where you stand. The consultation is free.

Sex Offense Allegations

Few accusations are more serious than this one. What's at stake is not just jail or prison time. It's your name, your family, your career, and depending on the charge, a lifetime on a public registry that follows you to every home, every job, and every relationship you'll ever have. For some offenses, a conviction can mean decades in prison. For many, registration lasts for life, long after any sentence ends.

And unlike other charges, the damage starts before any conviction. An accusation alone can cost you your job, your reputation, and time with your own children. That's why these cases cannot wait, and cannot be handled quietly on hope.

These cases are defended with detail, preparation, and care. We take the State's case apart piece by piece, and we take the time to understand what actually happened and who is involved. Nothing gets accepted at face value, because in these cases, the difference between an accusation and the truth is everything.

If you've been contacted, investigated, or charged, call now. Everything you tell us is confidential. The consultation is free.

Probation Violations

If the State has filed a motion to revoke your probation or adjudicate your deferred, you need to act now. These cases move fast, and if you're on deferred adjudication, the full punishment range for the original charge is back on the table.

But a motion filed is not a motion granted. Violations can be fought, explained, or resolved, and judges weigh the whole picture, not just the allegation. The sooner we're involved, the more we can do. Call today. The consultation is free.

Occupational Driver's Licenses

If your license has been suspended, life doesn't stop. You still have to get to work, pick up your kids, and make it to the store. An occupational driver's license lets you legally drive for work, school, and essential household duties while your suspension is in effect.

Whatever caused the suspension, we handle the process from start to finish. Don't risk driving while suspended, because getting caught makes everything worse. One phone call gets it moving. The consultation is free.

Expunctions and Non-Disclosures

Your case ended, but it still shows up on background checks. If your case was dismissed, you were found not guilty, or you completed deferred adjudication, you may be able to clear or seal your record. An expunction erases it. A non-disclosure seals it from public view.

Most people who qualify never do it, simply because they don't know they can. One free phone call tells you whether you qualify and what it takes.
