Driving While Intoxicated (DWI/DUI)

This refers to being accused of driving a vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs. It can range from a first‑time ticket to more serious charges if someone was hurt.

Drug Crimes

Covers offenses related to controlled substances, such as possession, distribution, manufacturing, trafficking, and even drug paraphernalia. These can be charged as misdemeanors or serious felonies depending on amount and intent.

Violent Crimes

These are crimes where someone is accused of hurting or threatening to hurt another person. Examples include assaults, fights, domestic violence, or more serious charges like manslaughter or murder.

Weapons Offenses

If someone is accused of illegally carrying, using, or owning a weapon (like a gun or knife), this falls under weapons crimes.

Juvenile Crimes

These are offenses committed by people under 18. The legal process is different from adult court and focuses more on rehabilitation.

Federal Crimes

Some crimes are charged under federal law (instead of just state law), like large‑scale drug trafficking, federal fraud, or offenses involving federal agencies. These usually come with harsher penalties.
